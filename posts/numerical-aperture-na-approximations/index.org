A short inquiry into NA approximations and their validity.

The definition for NA is given by the formula \[\mathrm{NA} = \sin{\theta}\] where
\(\theta\) is half the opening angle of the light cone accepted by the imaging
system.

For small angles the paraxial approximation \(\sin{\theta} = \theta\) can be used, for
calculating \(\theta\) and for calculating \(\sin{\theta}\).

For a thin lens \(\theta = \tan{\frac{D}{2f}}\), where \(D\) and \(f\) are
respectively the diameter and the focal length of the lens.
